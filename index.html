About

I'm a computational nuclear engineer with a Ph.D. from the University of Michigan, where my research focused on atomistic simulations of materials in radiation environments. My graduate work centered on developing and applying high-performance computing tools to study how nuclear materials evolve over time — work motivated by questions around long-term reactor reliability. My background spans molecular dynamics, kinetic Monte Carlo, and parallel computing, with experience running large-scale simulations on national HPC clusters. I've also had the opportunity to intern at Sandia National Laboratories, contribute to peer-reviewed research, and share my work at international conferences. I'm most at home at the intersection of physical modeling and scientific software development.
